Education Community Services

Education

Structured learning programs for children, youth, families, and serious students of Islamic knowledge.

Al Mizan Academy

A close-knit weekend academy founded through ICM in 2011 to connect youth with Islamic teachings, Quranic Arabic, and leadership skills they can use at home, school, and in the community.

  • Grades KG through high school
  • Quranic Arabic reading, writing, and memorization
  • Aqeedah, Islamic studies, history, and applied leadership
  • Homework shared weekly by email
Sundays, 10:05 AM - 1:20 PM

Contact: almizaan@icmnc.org

Nibraas Institute

A higher-learning track focused on Arabic, Quran, Hadith, theology, fiqh, classical texts, and scholarship in a serious community learning environment.

  • Four-year Islamic studies program
  • Eight semesters with in-person learning opportunities
  • Classes three times weekly for one hour
  • Taught by scholars with deep study in their fields
Multi-year Islamic studies track

Program contacts: imam@icmnc.org, khizrkazmi@icmnc.org

Al-Falah Quran School

A Quran-focused school for students and families seeking regular support in recitation, memorization, Tajweed, and stronger connection to the Book of Allah.

  • Registration-based Quran learning
  • Recitation and memorization support
  • Family-centered Islamic learning environment
  • Future backend should include registration forms
Registration-based program

Community Programs

Practical programs for youth, families, worshipers, volunteers, and neighbors in need.

Youth Programs

Weekly youth gatherings help students build faith, friendships, confidence, and a healthy connection to the masjid.

  • Elementary halaqa on Fridays
  • Middle and high school halaqas on Fridays
  • Games, sports, outings, and service opportunities
Youth gatherings and outings

Imam's Classes

Imam Manzar ul Islam al Azhari leads Quran classes, community reminders, khatiras, and regular learning opportunities for the masjid.

  • Quran classes in the musallah
  • Short reminders after Fajr and Isha
  • Community guidance and Islamic learning
Classes and khatiras

Social & Welfare Services

The social welfare work supports charity, brotherhood, family care, bridge-building, and service to neighbors. Zakat and sadaqah support should be distributed to eligible community members according to Islamic guidelines.

  • Food pantry support
  • Financial aid pathways
  • Family counseling and other services
  • Interfaith and neighborhood service
Service and welfare support

Contact: socialwelfare@icmnc.org
